My name is Tsogzolmaa. Currently I am a Cartography Masters student at ELTE (Eötvös Loránd University). I have 4 years of experience in processing satellite images, analyzing spatial data, and mapping topographic maps for large and medium scales. I am seeking an opportunity to apply my skills and experience in the field of GIS.

In 2023 we visited the town of Vác in Hungary.

Vác is located 35 kilometres north of Budapest on the eastern bank of the Danube river, below the bend where the river changes course and flows south. The town is seated at the foot of the Naszály Mountain in the foothills of the Carpathians.

The town of Vác dates back almost a thousand years. Due to the favorable natural conditions, the area has naturally been continuously inhabited for thousands of years. The hills, the forest, and the river are good opportunities for obtaining food, creating safe places to live, water transportation, the possibility of crossing the Danube, and the intersection of routes are a good background for trade.
